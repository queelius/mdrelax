thesis:
  claim: "When C2 (non-informative masking) is violated but assumed to hold, the MLE converges to a pseudo-true parameter whose individual component rates absorb the masking asymmetry, while the system hazard remains consistently estimated. Since the masking mechanism and component parameters are jointly non-identifiable, sensitivity analysis — not model elaboration — is the appropriate response to C2 uncertainty."
  novelty: "First systematic characterization of C2 misspecification bias in masked series systems. Proves system-hazard robustness and component-level degradation via pseudo-true parameter theory. Introduces a Bernoulli perturbation model for generating controlled C2 violations with calibrated severity.
